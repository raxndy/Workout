▸ FAILURE PROTOCOL — Isolation exercises: go to absolute muscular failure. Compounds: stop at technical failure (form breakdown = stop). This protects joints while maximising hypertrophic stimulus.
Training principles
AMRAP working sets
Every working set is performed as many reps as possible within the target zone. Stop when form breaks — not before.
Warm-up mandatory
3 warm-up sets on the first compound of each day. 1 warm-up set on subsequent exercises. Never skip — cold muscles fail early.
Progressive overload
Hit the top of the rep range on your last working set? Add weight next session. Use the same movement pattern — don't jump to a harder variation.
Rest times
Compounds: 2–3 min between working sets. Isolation: 60–90 sec. Between warm-ups: 45 sec. Cutting rest short costs reps at failure.
Weekly schedule
5-day rotating schedule
Mon: Push A · Tue: Pull A · Wed: Rest · Thu: Legs · Fri: Push B · Sat: Pull B · Sun: Rest — then repeat.
Why once-a-week legs
Squats and RDLs hit every lower muscle. Combined with the high volume and failure protocol, one full session per week is sufficient stimulus — and allows proper recovery.

Rest times
Between warm-up sets
45 seconds. No more. They're not taxing — don't rest like they are.
Between compound working sets
2–3 minutes. Creatine replenishment takes ~2 min. Cutting this short directly costs reps on the next set.
Between isolation working sets
60–90 seconds. Isolation muscles recover faster. Keeping rest tighter here adds training density.
Between exercises
2–3 minutes when moving to a new compound. 90 sec when moving to isolation. Use this time to set up the next station.

Supplements
Evidence-ranked. Nothing without a reason.
Essential
Creatine monohydrate
Most researched ergogenic available. Directly increases phosphocreatine stores — more ATP for those extra reps at failure. This is non-negotiable when training to failure. Effect: +1–2kg lean mass over 4–12 weeks vs placebo.
▸ 5g daily, any time. No loading phase. Takes 3–4 weeks to saturate.
Essential
Whey protein
Only if you can't reliably hit 160–180g protein from food alone. Leucine-rich and fast-digesting. Food first — this is a convenience tool.
▸ 1 scoop post-workout or between meals. 25–30g protein per serving.
Essential
Caffeine (coffee)
Since you're training to failure, pre-workout caffeine directly increases the number of reps you can hit at failure. Skip the pre-workout powder — black coffee is cheaper, cleaner, and equally effective.
▸ 3–6mg/kg bodyweight, 45 min before training. ~200–300mg for your bodyweight.
Optional
Omega-3 (EPA/DHA)
Anti-inflammatory properties relevant to your shoulder history. May also support MPS signalling at higher intakes. Especially worth it given your injury background.
▸ 2–3g EPA+DHA daily with food. Fish oil or algae-based.
Optional
Vitamin D3 + K2
Relevant if you're indoors most of the day. Supports testosterone, immune function, and bone density. Most people in Egypt get adequate sun — but worth checking if you work indoors all day.
▸ 2000–4000 IU D3 + 100mcg K2, daily with a fatty meal.
Skip
BCAAs · Fat burners · Pre-workout powders · Mass gainers
BCAAs are redundant with adequate protein. Fat burners are ineffective or unsafe. Pre-workouts are overpriced caffeine. Mass gainers are just expensive oats with sugar. None of these move the needle beyond what's listed above.
Progression Map
12-week structured cycle with deload and reassessment.
WEEKS 1–2 // REACCLIMATION
Re-establish failure thresholds
Your PRs are from your last peak. Start at ~85% of those numbers and work into failure. Your tendons and connective tissue need 2 weeks to catch up to your muscle strength. Don't ego-load on week 1.
WEEKS 3–6 // ACCUMULATION
Push PRs, add weight when ready
When you hit the top of the rep range on your last working set with good form, add weight next session. Compounds: +2.5–5kg. Isolation: +2.5kg or next cable pin. Log every set — this phase is where the bulk of muscle is built.
WEEKS 7–8 // INTENSIFICATION
Drop sets and mechanical drop sets
On the last working set of 1–2 isolation exercises per session, add a drop set: immediately reduce weight by 20–30% and go to failure again. This is optional but effective for adding volume at the end of a set without extra rest time.
WEEKS 9–11 // PEAK
Max volume, push every set
Add 1 extra working set to the primary compound of each session if recovery is good. Sleep and appetite are your recovery signals — if either degrades, don't add sets.
WEEK 12 // DELOAD
50% volume, same weights
Halve the working sets. Keep the weight identical. This is mandatory — accumulated fatigue masks fitness. The deload lets your nervous system and connective tissue recover so adaptations can express. You'll come back stronger.
ONGOING // TRACKING
What to log and when
Every session: exercise name · weight · warm-up sets · working sets · reps at failure. Weekly: 3-day average morning weight (fasted). Monthly: tape measurements — chest, arms flexed, waist, thighs. Target: +0.3–0.5kg/week bodyweight. Strength should increase every 1–2 weeks on at least one exercise per session.
